User-Stories

User-stories provide a simple, easy to read, articulation of what users of the tresplace app can do. They are the reference document for what needs to be included in the MVP (mininum viable product).

Note that there was extensive discussion around the revenue model for tresplace. Some advocated for facilitating the purchase of coffee drinks through app or a monthly plan or tab service to users' 'loved' coffee shop. There is a counter point that this is too much to accomplish for the MVP. More discussion to be had on this topic moving forward...

AS a Coffee Creator, I can ...

  • make a 'Pourtrait' of my coffee shop
  • upload high quality images to my shop pourtrait
  • upload high quality video to my shop pourtrait
  • create an investigation* for my shop pourtrait
  • show investigation results infographically and textually
  • customize my investigation or choose a pre-packaged one
  • include questions and/or modules in my investigation
  • be seduced to fill out my pourtrait with sexy tresPlace UI
  • post my active menu of coffee drinks
  • post events
  • post promotional pricing
  • monitor and remove user-generated content

As Coffee Lovers we can ...

  • request that a coffee creator builds an Investigation
  • create my own investigation
  • 'like' many coffee shops and drinks
  • 'love' only one coffee shop and one drink
  • enable our own lover-pourtrait
  • show our relationship status to coffee drinks and shops
  • display the investigations we've filled out
  • share our investigation results through social media
  • create our own investigations for the shops we 'love'
  • read a lovers & creators - feed
  • see our shop relationship status and that of others
  • see our social media frends pourtraits
  • can write love letters to our fave coffee shops

Wireframe Mocks

Wireframes are used to show the basic layout and flow of an apps user-experience and user-interface. The user-interface and user-experience are crucial to the success of an app and should be easy-to-use, fun, intuitive, engaging, encourage the user to use it over and over again.

This was a creativity exercise to get the tresplace team thinking along these lines.

Next Steps...

At this time, we have: a strong concept of the product, the market opportunity is laid out, and the product's basic functionality is sketched. It's time to move onto the next steps.

The beginnings of the product band are emerging, and an identity which will unite the tresplace team and attract financial and creative resources in the startup market is called for. A solid brand can inspire the creative process, provide a sense of purpose and unity within the team, and create a sexy face for the project.

Branding is a suggested next step in the tresplace development journey.
